Over the years I felt that off the shelf keyboards weren't very good so I started looking into alternatives. Now I am in a 6 mile deep pit and I have traded my soul with the eldritch ergonomic beast for a slightly better typing experience.

Now everyone thinks I am an esoteric freak that scoffs at everyone else because they use membrane keyboards instead of glorious [ortholinear](https://helpdeskgeek.com/what-are-ortholinear-keyboards-and-should-you-get-one/) split keyboards!!!

When I sit down and pull out my sick ass keyboard, instantly, everyone assumes that I am either Salvador Dalí reincarnate or that I am about to open 7 terminals and do something unexplainable.  

I can't use normal keyboards anymore and if you put a QWERTY keyboard in front of me you will think it's my first time using a computer.

My life is forever ruined and it's all because I wanted to make my keyboard orange instead of black!

To help explain how I ended up here I'll give you the rundown of my stupid keyboard journey in bullet form:
- Corsair keycaps letters smudged off so I got new keycaps.
- Didn't use the numpad and wanted more desk space so I got a 60% keyboard.
- I don't like clicky loud keys so built and soldered a 60% keyboard with MX Brown switches (GROSS!)
- Felt as if I was having to hold my hands tight together and stumbled upon split keyboards and their ergonomic benefit (life ruined forever).
- Fell into a deep pit regarding ergonomics and now I sleep on my back because of keyboards.
- Got a DIY ErgoDox and built it because the other options were too expensive. I got linear switches to try out instead of tactile and was happy with the results.
- Mangled the PCB while building it so now if I pressed a certain key it would type the entire row.
- Got a second hand herman miller aeron chair as I am now horribly aware of sitting and spine health. Did not spend 1500GBP, got it for 200 (very cheap and cool).
- Lived with my badly made keyboard for a year and then upgraded to a ["Hot Dox" Ergodox V2"]([https://apos.audio/cdn/shop/files/apos-audio-alpaca-keyboards-mechanical-keyboards-ergodox-76-hot-dox-v2-mechanical-keyboard-39939249209580.png?v=1763086804&width=1200](https://jonathanbayless.com/2019/09/16/hotdox-review.html) to avoid the pain of building another keyboard.
- Decided to get silent linear key switches and now my keyboard does not annoy anyone within a 20cm radius (including me).
- Felt like QWERTY was not a good layout and looked to find something that was more ergonomic.
- Settled on a modified [ISRT](https://www.keyboard-design.com/letterlayout.html?layout=isrt-ts-202103.en.ergodox) layout.
- Really like how the layout makes my fingers feel like they are working in harmony.
- Realized I don't use most of my keys on my ErgoDox.
- Went down a rabbit hole and found out there hundreds of split keyboard alternative layouts.
- Find a cool layout called [Corne](https://github.com/foostan/crkbd)
- Really hated having to solder tiny diodes onto PCBs to create a [Matrix mapping](https://techschematic.com/keyboard-matrix-wiring) of the keys.
- Found out there's a way to bypass this by mapping all the keys directly to each pin of the micro controller from the [Cantor](https://github.com/diepala/cantor) layout.
- Realize that open source keyboard layouts are just iterations of other layouts.
- Find yet ANOTHER layout that is an iteration of Cantor that is wireless and dirt cheap to build.
- [Maizeless my beloved](https://github.com/BlueDrink9/maizeless)
- Learn how to design PCBs because I wanted to make some changes of my own.
- Design the PCB, case and keycaps.
- Order everything
- Build it
- Create the and flash the layout using [ZMK](https://zmk.dev/) 

After all of this I now have a wireless, portable split keyboard that I can use on any device. It is small enough to take anywhere and is quiet enough to not annoy anyone. The best part though? it's designed around me!

I have simplified my keyboard to the point that it is only 42 keys (21 per hand) and I can navigate any application or device without issue. 

Being comfortable when using a computer has been my main goal in all of this and I've always thought that there had to be something better than the black dell keyboard I keep getting handed. I don't think you should have to spend a small fortune to get a better typing experience but the bad part about falling into this pit is that no one can tell you what you would like; you just have to go and try stuff out.

You don't have to design a keyboard and create your own layout to find what works for you. Just go and get something off the shelf and go from there. I don't even type faster than I did when I was using a full-sized qwerty keyboard. However; I am much happier with it!
